Xiaodong Cheng - About

Bio

  • I received the B.S. and M.S. degrees in School of Electronics and Information from Northwestern Polytechnical University, Xi'an, China, in 2011 and 2014, respectively. From Sept. 2014 to Dec. 2018, I was a PhD student in the Jan C. Willems Center for Systems and Control, Engineering and Technology Institute Groningen, at the Faculty of Science and Engineering, University of Groningen, the Netherlands, where I worked on the topic “Model reduction of network systems with structure preservation”, under the supervision of Prof. Jacquelien M. A. Scherpen. I received my Ph.D. degree (distinction cum laude) in Nov. 2018. Since Jan. 2019, I have moved to the control systems group, Department of Electrical Engineering at the Eindhoven University of Technology, the Netherlands, where I am currently a postdoc working on the project “Data-Driven Modeling in Dynamic Networks”, under the supervision of Prof. Paul M.J. Van den Hof. I am the recipient of the Paper Prize Award from the IFAC Journal Automatica for the best paper published in the triennium 2017-2019 and the 2020 IEEE Transactions on Control Systems Technology Outstanding Paper Award.

  • I have been a IEEE member since 2013 and a member of the Dutch Institute of Systems and Control (DISC) since 2014.

  • Broadly, my research lies in the direction of modeling and control in networked systems, which have a wide range of applications including power grids, networked robots, and biochemical reactions. I am particularly interested in there topics in dynamic networks: reduced-order modelling (model reduction), data-driven modelling (system identification), and distributed/decentralized control.
