About Me

I am an assistant professor with the Dynamic Systems, Signals, and Control group at Biometris in the Department of Plant Science, Wageningen University & Research (WUR).

Before joining WUR, I was appointed as a research associate in the Department of Engineering at the University of Cambridge from 2020 to 2022 and a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Electrical Engineering at the Eindhoven University of Technology from 2019 to 2020. I received my B.S. and M.E. degrees from Northwestern Polytechnical University, China, in 2011 and 2014, respectively, and in November 2018, I was awarded a Ph.D. degree with honors (cum laude) from the University of Groningen, The Netherlands, under the supervision of Prof. Jacquelien M. A. Scherpen. I am the recipient of the Paper Prize Award from the IFAC Journal Automatica in the triennium 2017-2019 and the Outstanding Paper Award from IEEE Transactions on Control Systems Technology in 2020.

My main research interests include model reduction, system identification, and control in large-scale networked systems. I am also keen on the analysis and synthesis of complex systems in real-world applications including agricultural systems, power networks, networked robots, and biochemical reactions.
